News ISPSC hosts IRCET 2023. Sep 15, 2023


Research is fully embraced by every higher education institution as one of the mandates it has to perform. Ilocos Sur Polytechnic State College (ISPSC) and Abra State Institute of Sciences and Technology (ASIST) perform this mandate and commit to imbibing the culture of research for educational transformation and development.

This collaborative activity, IRCET 2023, being undertaken by ISPSC and ASIST provides academic institutions and industries the opportunities for intellectual discussion and dissemination of research outputs through presentations. Through this activity, scholars, researchers, and professionals will have the great opportunity to share ideas and experiences that will eventually contribute to educational and technological advancement and development.

The 1st International Conference on Education and Technology (IRCET) 2023 which will take place on October 12-13 via Zoom Teleconferencing serves as an academic platform for researchers, professionals, scholars, academicians, and graduate students to present their research findings, the latest educational developments, and emerging technologies. This conference primarily showcases the latest discoveries in the disciplines.

Back-to-Back Win! ISPSC’s bet crowned Ms. ASCU-SN 2023


In a stunning demonstration of agility, intellect, and resilience, Miss Delrio Dumawa, an enthusiastic faculty member from Ilocos Sur Polytechnic State College-Main Campus, emerged victorious as Ms. ASCU-SN 2023 during the Search for Mr. & Ms. ASCU-SN 2023 held at Tarlac Agricultural University on June 28, 2023.

The Mr. & Ms. ASCU-SN pageant is a highly anticipated event that aims to foster camaraderie, celebrate the beauty and intelligence of the contestants, and promote strong relationships among higher education institutions in the Northern Philippines.

This year's theme, "Sulong Norte: Solidifying Northern Relationships through Games of Agility and Ingenuity," focused on embracing progress, unity, and collaboration among educational institutions through various games and challenges designed to test the contestants' agility and ingenuity.

The highlight of Miss Dumawa's journey to victory was her eloquent response during the question-and-answer portion. She emphasized the crucial role of resilience in overcoming adversity, highlighting the unity that emerged among the students, faculty, and staff as they stood together to support one another during this difficult time. Their collective determination to continue pursuing their dreams and upholding the university's mission showcased the strength and character of ISPSC.

Dumawa's victory as Ms. ASCU-SN 2023 symbolizes the power of resilience and unity in the face of adversity. It solidifies the institution's reputation as a powerhouse of talent, intelligence and resilience in the Northern Philippines. Undeniably, her success serves as an inspiration to others, reminding everyone of the incredible heights that can be reached through unwavering perseverance and the support of a strong institution.

This back-to-back win at the Ms. ASCU-SN pageant serves as a testament to the commitment and dedication of the faculty and students of ISPSC. The institution's focus on holistic education, empowering students and faculty to excel academically while nurturing their talents has proven to be a winning formula.

ISPSC ranks in 2023 Global Top Innovative Universities. May 19, 2023


With its innovative programs on student mobility and openness, crisis management, and the fourth industrial revolution, the Ilocos Sur Polytechnic State College (ISPSC) was ranked as one of the Global Top Innovative Universities in the May 2023 ranking of The World University Rankings for Innovation (WURI).

ISPSC's internationalization program, Project MARING, and Luntiang Pamayanan community extension projects have made the College's ranking in the global top 201-300 universities possible.

As part of the president's agendum for global positioning, ISPSC is opening its borders for student and faculty exchange and collaborative faculty research undertakings with various universities in Indonesia, Taiwan, and the USA, and expanding further to other countries like India.

Initially started as a virtual exchange program, ISPSC and its partner universities in Indonesia adopted the student exchange with credit, visiting professor, and research and extension collaboration initiatives, giving students and visiting professors a one-of-a-kind experience in international collaboration.

Dr. Annabelle B. Francisco, Internationalization and Networking Officer, initiated the said collaboration efforts, with the help and support of the campus internationalization coordinators and the entire ISPSC administration.

Meanwhile, Project MARING was ISPSC's way of responding to the call of its immediate community during the onslaught of Typhoon Maring in Ilocos Sur that swept away properties, including animals and houses, leaving many people homeless and devastated.

Through the effort of the ISPSC community, led by Dr. Gilbert R. Arce, SUC President III, several families benefitted from the housing rehabilitation project and several others in the offering of free drinking water through the ISPSC Water Station, feeding activities, donation drives, and other initiatives conducted by the College for several weeks to help mitigate the effects of the typhoon.

On the other hand, the Luntiang Pamayanan extension project of the College of Agriculture, Forestry, Engineering, and Development Communication (CAFED) promotes urban and backyard gardening to help attain food security in the communities, especially those highly affected by the pandemic, and empower residents to produce food for self-sustainability with utmost consideration on environmental protection through the utilization of waste as organic fertilizer.

Spearheaded by Dr. Armando D. Diga, CAFED Dean, the Luntiang Pamayanan project has now benefitted more than 200 households, 17 communities, and 30 schools in the second district of Ilocos Sur. Capacity-building activities on food sustainability and security for 137 participants were also conducted.

With WURI's specificity on the university's real impact on society through creative and innovative approaches, the inclusion of ISPSC in the world ranking has, indeed, marked its continuing effort to place the quality and relevance of higher education in the country, with high regard for student mobility and openness, social responsibility, crisis management, progress, value-creating, and industry application, among others. #

ISPSC undergoes re-certification for Quality Management System


The Ilocos Sur Polytechnic State College (ISPSC) underwent re-certification for Quality Management System by SOCOTEC International on May 9-10, 2023.

The audit team from SOCOTEC International composed of Engineer Eugene Herradura, Engineer Laureen Grace Calmerin, Engineer Michael Matundan, Engineer Jet Diel, Engineer Fatima Mercurio, and Engineer Billy Sadangsal.

Processes to support the provision of of educational services covering teaching, learning, and support processes were the areas that were audited.

In the closing meeting, the audit team mentioned, "Therefore, Ilocos Sur Polytechnic State College will be recommended for re-certification for Quality Management System..."

"Everything paid off. What was thought of to be impossible is now a reality. Thank you to all my colleagues for their dedication, sacrifices, and unwavering support! Thank you especially to our colleagues in the former NLPSC, now the ISPSC Main Campus, for embracing the challenge and for their commitment and desire to be ISO Certified!! Now, ISPSC is RE-CERTIFIED with the inclusion of the Main Campus!!! ... But one sure thing remains - ISPSC is CERTIFIED to be a quality institution," Dr. Gilbert R. Arce, SUC President III, articulated on his facebook post.#

CHED-RO1 visits ISPSC

ISPSC Delegates attend 6th Microbiome R&D, Business Collaboration Congress


Tokyo, Japan-–The delegations of the Ilocos Sur Polytechic State College headed by Dr. Gilbert R. Arce, SUC President III, attended the 6th Microbiome R&D and Business Collaboration Congress on April 26-27, 2023.

This congress is also co-located with Asia 5th Probiotics & Prebiotics Congress, Asia 4th Skin Microbiome & Cosmeceuticals Congress, and joint probiotics symposium ’23. The said event is organized by Global Engage Ltd, and co-hosted with the Japanese Society of Probiotic Science (JSPS).

Dr. Remy Sanidad (ISPSC’s Vice President for Public Information, Research, and Extension) and Ms. Charlene A. Ballesteros (faculty researcher) also attended the said congress.

“The event will embrace fundamental and applied aspects scientifically and commercially including topic interests ranging from microbiome communities, how the microbiome affects health, disease, and the therapeutic industry, skin-based research, cosmetic advancements, probiotics, prebiotics postbiotics, brain health, academic-commercial partnerships, among others,” Yasuhiro Koga, President of Japanese Society for Probiotic Science, mentioned on his posted letter of invitation.#
